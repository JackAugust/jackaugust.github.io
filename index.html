Jie Zhang
Ph.D candidate in Computer Science and Technology
School of Cybersecurity, Tianjin University

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science and Technology at Tianjin University, China, supervised by Prof. Xiaohong Li. My research focuses on blockchain data security sharing and privacy preservation, with a specific interest in the integration of cryptographic techniques for secure access control and data governance.

I has published over 10 scientific papers in international journals and conference proceedings, such as TDSC, TSC, IoT-J, CSCWD, TASE, with one paper recognized as Highly Cited/Downloaded. I holds 5 authorized invention patents and 6 registered software copyrights. I has served as the program committee member for two important international conferences and the reviewer for nine prestigious journals, including TDSC, TIFS, et al. I has served as a key member in national-level projects, such as the National Key R&D Program and the National Natural Science Foundation of China.

What I am exploring: I am actively expanding my research into the intersection of AI and Security (AI4Security), investigating emerging risks and novel challenges in AI systems, while continuing to advance privacy-preserving blockchain data security sharing and access control. I warmly welcome collaboration opportunities with researchers and students who share these interests.

What I am seeking: I am actively looking for faculty/research positions (tenure-track or research-oriented) that align with my expertise in blockchain privacy, access control, and AI security. If my background fits your team, please don't hesitate to reach out.

Get in touch: Feel free to contact me at jackzhang@tju.edu.cn.
