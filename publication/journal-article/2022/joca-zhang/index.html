‘Access control model for Internet of Things (IoT) based on blockchain and edge computing’

Jie Zhang, Shanshan Xu, Lingyun Yuan(*)
April 2021
PDF Project Project DOI

Abstract

The emergence of edge computing extends the scope of the cloud-terminal architecture of Internet of Things (IoT), and reduces the transmission and processing latency of massive data of terminal devices while bringing new security issues. Aiming at the data security and management problems between the edge nodes of Internet of Things (IoT) and massive heterogeneous devices, and considering the current blockchain technology is widely used for the security management of data in distributed systems, the Access control model for Internet of Things (IoT) based on blockchain and edge computing (SC-ABAC) is proposed. Firstly, the access control architecture of Internet of Things (IoT) with integrated edge computing is proposed, and SC-ABAC is proposed and designed by combining smart contract and attribute-based access control, followed by the optimisation of the traditional consensus algorithm (PoW) and the access control management process of SC-ABAC. Finally, the experimental results show that in this paper, under continuous access to blocks, the time consumption increases linearly with the number of times, and the central processor (CPU) utilisation is stable during continuous access with good security. Under this model, only the query process exists the time consumption of invoking the contract grows linearly with the number of times, the time consumption of the policy addition and judgement process are of constant level, and the optimized consensus mechanism reduces the time consumption of the consensus by about 18.37 percentage points per 100 blocks compared to PoW. The model provides decentralised, fine-grained and dynamic Access control management in Internet of Things (IoT) environments, and faster consensus to ensure data consistency in distributed systems.
