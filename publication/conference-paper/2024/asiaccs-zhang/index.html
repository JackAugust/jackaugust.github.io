SwiftGuard: Swift and Secure Attribute-Based Access Control Scalable to Cross-Domain Emergency Collaboration

Mengke zhang, Xiaohong Li, Jie Zhang, Zhe Hou, Guangdong Bai, Ruitao Feng
September 2024
Project

Abstract

Ensuring secure and efficient cross-domain emergency collaboration remains a major challenge in the evolving smart healthcare space. Traditional access control models often struggle to address the inherent tension between fine-grained Access control, fast response time, and security and privacy protection, especially across autonomous healthcare domains in emergency situations. In this paper, we introduce SwiftGuard, a novel access control system designed for healthcare environments that scales to urgent cross-domain collaboration during emergency care.SwiftGuard leverages an entropy-based context-aware token access control approach to enable dynamic fine-grained access control while maintaining the speed required for emergency response. It also employs a zero-knowledge scope proofing protocol for secure pre-authentication and filtering of collaborators, ensuring that cross-domain data sharing meets strict privacy and security standards. The master-slave chaining architecture preserves domain autonomy while facilitating secure cross-domain collaboration. Security analyses validate the controllability of Access control, emphasising the zero-knowledge and robustness of proof-of-scope protocols for cross-domain processes. Experimental results show that Access control time complexity is reduced from $O(n)$ to $O(\log n)$, improving throughput and reducing latency to milliseconds. These features make SwiftGuard an effective solution for the healthcare industry, balancing fast data access with robust security in cross-domain emergency collaboration.
